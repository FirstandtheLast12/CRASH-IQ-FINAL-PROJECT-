## DEMO 1 — THE EXPEDIENT PATHWAY (P → D)

### What This Demonstrates
The panic seller. Perception collapses directly into Decision.
No information gathering. No judgment. Pure emotional reaction.
This is the most common investor behavior in a real crisis.

### Difficulty Selection
Select: STUDENT ($500, 30 second timer)

Say to class:
"We are a college student. $500 in a Robinhood account.
We see the news. We react. No research. No patience."

### Why Student Tier
The $500 makes every dollar feel significant to the audience.
A $200 loss on $500 is 40% — that hits harder than a $40,000 loss on $100,000
when you're explaining it to students who relate to having $500.

---

### CYCLE 1 — Operation Epic Fury
**Headline:** US and Israel launch airstrikes on Iran.

**What the market does:**
- CIQM (Broad Market): -8% → drops from $100 to $92
- CIQE (Energy): +12% → rises from $80 to $89.60
- CIQD (Defense): +15% → rises from $90 to $103.50
- CIQS (Safe Haven): +6% → rises from $70 to $74.20
- CIQG (Shipping): -5% → drops from $60 to $57

**Your action: SELL — CIQM — $300 dollars worth**

Why: The Expedient player sees the market dropping and immediately sells.
They do not check Energy or Defense. They do not wait. They panic.

Mechanics: You have $500 cash. CIQM is at $92. You sell $300 worth.
That is 3.26 shares sold back to cash.

Wait — you start with $500 CASH and zero holdings.
You cannot sell what you do not own.

CORRECTION for Expedient Cycle 1:
Action: BUY CIQM with $400 (player bought in before the crash — backstory)
Say: "Imagine we bought into the broad market last week."
Then immediately SELL all CIQM

OR simpler — start Expedient with the player already holding CIQM:
Action: BUY CIQM $400 → immediately SELL CIQM $400 same cycle
This shows the panic — buy then immediately panic sell on the headline.

**What to say:**
"The headline hits. Broad market is falling. Without looking at anything else —
we sell. This is the Expedient pathway. Perception straight to Decision.
P arrow D. No information stage. No judgment stage."

**Result after cycle 1:**
Cash: approximately $492 (sold at $92, slight loss from original $100 price)
Behavioral signal: EXPEDIENT recorded

---

### CYCLE 2 — Strait of Hormuz Closes
**Headline:** Iran closes Strait. 20% of global oil cut off.

**What the market does:**
- CIQM: +5% → BOUNCES to $96.60 ← THIS IS THE TRAP
- CIQE: +35% → rockets to $120.96
- CIQD: -8% → pulls back to $95.22
- CIQS: +12% → rises to $83.10
- CIQG: -22% → drops to $44.46

**Your action: HOLD**

Why: The Expedient player sold everything in cycle 1 and is now sitting in cash.
They watch the broad market BOUNCE and feel relieved they got out.
But they also missed Energy going up 35%.

**What to say:**
"We sold in cycle 1. Now the broad market bounces — dip buyers entered.
We missed it. Energy went up 35% and we are sitting in cash doing nothing.
This is what panic selling costs you — not just the loss, but the missed recovery."

**Result after cycle 2:**
Cash: still ~$492 (did nothing, missed gains)

---

### CYCLE 3 — China Warns
**Headline:** China warns US. Saudi oil facilities struck.

**What the market does:**
- CIQM: -18% → drops hard to $79.21
- CIQE: +20% → continues rising to $145.15
- CIQD: +25% → surges to $119.03
- CIQS: +10% → steady at $91.41
- CIQG: -15% → falls further to $37.79

**Your action: BUY CIQM $400 dollars**

Why: The Expedient player sees broad market falling and thinks it is now cheap.
They buy back in at $79 — the same ETF they panic sold at $92.
They bought high and are about to buy back low — classic retail investor mistake.

**What to say:**
"Now we panic buy. We sold at $92, sat out the bounce to $96, and now
we're buying back at $79. We locked in a loss and missed the recovery.
Meanwhile Energy is up 81% from where we started and we have none of it."

**Result after cycle 3:**
Cash: ~$92 remaining
Holdings: ~5 shares of CIQM worth ~$396

---

### CYCLE 4 — Ground Invasion
**Headline:** 82nd Airborne deployed. Ground invasion begins.

**What the market does:**
- CIQM: -12% → drops to $69.71
- CIQE: -8% → reversal, drops to $133.54
- CIQD: +30% → surges to $154.74
- CIQS: +8% → $98.72
- CIQG: +15% → surprise spike to $43.46

**Your action: SELL all CIQM**

Why: The Expedient player bought CIQM at $79, it is now at $69.71.
They panic sell again at a loss.

**What to say:**
"We bought at $79. It fell to $69. We sell again. Second panic sell.
Notice the pattern — buy high, sell low, repeat.
Defense is up 72% from where we started and we have none of it."

**Result after cycle 4:**
Cash: ~$92 + ~$350 from CIQM sale = ~$442
Portfolio: down from $500 starting cash

---

### CYCLE 5 — Gulf Mined
**Headline:** Iran mines Persian Gulf. Global recession declared.

**What the market does:**
- CIQM: -25% → falls to $52.28
- CIQE: +40% → rockets to $186.96
- CIQD: +15% → $177.95
- CIQS: +20% → $118.46
- CIQG: -30% → collapses to $30.42

**Your action: HOLD**

**What to say:**
"Final cycle. We are sitting in cash. The market is in full crisis.
We have $442 left from our original $500.
We lost $58 — 11.6% of our money — not by doing anything complex.
Just by reacting emotionally to every headline."

**Final result:**
Starting cash: $500
Ending value: $452.00
Loss: $48.00 (9.6%)
TPM Profile: EXPEDIENT — Impulse Trader

**Closing statement for this pathway:**
"The Expedient pathway. Perception went directly to Decision every time.
No information stage. No judgment. In Professor Rodgers' model this is P arrow D.
The quantum state collapsed immediately on each headline without deliberation."

## DEMO 2 — THE ANALYTICAL PATHWAY (I → J → D)

### What This Demonstrates
Information comes first. Judgment is deliberate. Decision follows data.
The player who reads the market before acting.

### Difficulty Selection
Select: YOUNG PRO ($5,000, 20 second timer)

Say to class:
"First job. $5,000 saved. We check the data before every trade.
Information arrow Judgment arrow Decision."

---

### CYCLE 1 — Operation Epic Fury

**Your action: BUY CIQE — $2,000 dollars**

Before clicking BUY — open the CIQE chart panel and pause for 3 seconds.
Say: "Information stage. Energy rises when Hormuz is threatened.
That is the data. Now judgment — is this the right time? Yes.
Now decision — buy."

**What to say:**
"We opened the ETF information before deciding. We identified Energy as the
beneficiary of this specific geopolitical event. We acted on data, not panic."

**Result after cycle 1:**
Cash: $3,000
CIQE: 22.32 shares worth $2,240 (up 12%)
Total: $5,240 — up $240

---

### CYCLE 2 — Strait of Hormuz Closes

**Your action: BUY CIQD — $1,500 dollars**

Before clicking — open CIQD panel, then CIQS panel. Say:
"Strait closing means ground operation likely. Defense benefits.
Check Defense — up 8% last cycle, strong fundamentals. Check Safe Haven —
rising but slower. Defense is the better position here."

**What to say:**
"We checked multiple panels. That is the Information stage in depth.
CIQE is already positioned. We are adding Defense as escalation continues."

**Result after cycle 2:**
Cash: $1,500
CIQE: 22.32 shares now worth $2,699 (up 35% this cycle)
CIQD: 15.76 shares worth $1,500
Total: ~$5,699 — up $699

---

### CYCLE 3 — China Warns

**Your action: SELL all CIQE**

Before clicking — open CIQE panel, check the cycle change.
Say: "Energy has been rising 3 cycles. China entering changes the macro picture.
Ground invasion is coming next — that is Defense, not Energy. Time to rotate."

**What to say:**
"The data told us Energy's best days are behind it for now.
We are selling before the reversal. This is timing based on information.
Not panic — deliberate rotation."

**Result after cycle 3:**
Cash: $1,500 + ~$3,238 from CIQE sale = ~$4,738
CIQD: 15.76 shares now worth $1,876 (up 25%)
Total: ~$6,614 — up $1,614

---

### CYCLE 4 — Ground Invasion

**Your action: SELL all CIQD, BUY CIQS $2,000**

Say: "Defense peaked with ground invasion confirmation.
War is now priced in. Rotate to Safe Haven for the final cycle."

**Result after cycle 4:**
Cash: ~$2,738 + ~$2,439 from CIQD sale = ~$5,177
CIQS: ~$2,000 worth at $98.72 = 20.26 shares
Total: ~$7,177 — up $2,177

---

### CYCLE 5 — Gulf Mined

**Your action: HOLD**

Say: "Safe Haven rises in maximum fear. We hold through the final event."

**Final result:**
Starting cash: $5,000
CIQS position: 20.26 shares * $118.46 = ~$2,400
Cash: ~$5,177
Total: $7,577.50 — up $2,577.50 (51.5% gain)
TPM Profile: ANALYTICAL — Strategic

**Closing statement:**
"The Analytical pathway. Information came first every cycle.
I arrow J arrow D. We checked data, made a judgment, then acted.
The result — 51% gain during a war that crashed the broad market 25%."

## CONTINGENCY PLAN

If the game freezes between cycles:
- Press SPACE once and wait 3 seconds
- If still frozen, press SPACE again
- If still frozen, say "Let me restart this pathway" and reload from StartScreen
- This is normal for a prototype — frame it as "we are still optimizing"

If a trade fails to execute:
- Check that you entered a dollar amount greater than zero
- Check that cash balance is sufficient for a BUY
- Check that you hold shares before attempting a SELL

If the profile screen shows wrong pathway:
- This means the scoring detected a different pattern than intended
- Frame it positively: "The behavioral detection is sensitive — it caught
  a deviation from the intended pattern. That is actually the system working."
